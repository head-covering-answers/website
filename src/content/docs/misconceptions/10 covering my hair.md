title: Does the head covering need to cover all of my hair?
description: Explaining the relationship between head covering and hair

Some women who practice head covering believe they are not permitted to have their hair showing when they wear a head covering.

Where does this perspective come from? Since wearing long hair is a glory (1 Corinthians 11:15), they believe that Paul's instructions are to keep it hidden in order to avoid it being a physical/visual distraction from the glory of God.

This is something associated with the mistaken that idea that visible hair is sensual and that a head covering is [intended for modesty.]


### ANSWER:  It is not necessary to hide all hair under a head covering.

### REASONS
- Repeatedly, Paul refers to the purpose of the garment as covering the "head" and not the "hair." The *head* is the focus of the physical symbol (to go along with the principle of headship). He never refers to it as a hair-covering.
- Paul expects that a woman's hair is visible externally, not internally hidden under a head covering (1 Timothy 2:9-10, 1 Peter 3:3-4).
- - In 1 Corinthians 11:15, Paul says that it is glory unto a woman to "wear long hair" (a Greek verb). Grammatically, the long hair itself (noun) isn't the glory, but the wearing of it is. Obviously, the verb and the noun are closely associated (the verb wouldn't be glorious if it weren't for the noun).
