:::tip[Overview]{icon="sun"}
- 1 Corinthians 11:10 says, "Therefore the woman ought to have a symbol of authority on her head, because of the angels."

- Many wonder about the phrase "because of the angels." The reference to angels is very brief and is not fully explained in the text.

- Some wonder if "angels" refers to literal spiritual beings, human messengers (like pastors), or even fallen angels who might be tempted by uncovered women.
:::

## Answer

- Paul states that head covering is appropriate "because of the angels." This is true, regardless if we understand it or not.

- By basing head covering on angels, Paul shows the practice is not a temporary cultural custom. Since angels transcend human culture and time, this reason for the symbol remains in effect for the church today.

- Though we may wish that additional explanation was provided, Paul simply doesn't exlain *how* angels are related to head covering. There are several potential explanations (see below).

<br>

***

## Explanation

1. Scripture may indicate that angels are unseen spiritual observers who watch what God does in His kingdom on earth (1 Corinthians 4:9, Ephesians 3:10, 1 Timothy 5:21, 1 Peter 1:12). So the use of head coverings may serve as a testimony to angels that Christian women are honoring God's directions and [glory](/glory).

2. Some high-ranking angels (the seraphim) cover themselves when they are in the presence of God’s glory (Isaiah 6:2). So perhaps there is a similarity in practice between God's women and God's angels.

4. Since 1 Corinthians 11 teaches that head covering is related to prayer, perhaps there is a connection with the angels' involvement in the prayers of the saints (Revelation 8:3-4).

5. The Greek word translated "angel" can also be translated as "messenger," which some believe could refer to pastors. Here, the spiritual headship of the church is connected with the headship concepts discussed in other parts of 1 Corinthians 11.
