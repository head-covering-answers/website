
**ABILITY**

Principle: You can choose to do hard things.

Examples:  I've had my finger pricked over 100 times.  I've cold plunged dozens of time.  I've done over 10,000 pushups in the last 9 months.  I've run two ultramarathons and two "century" (100+ mile) bike rides.


**POWER OF COMFORT**

Principle:  You avoid healthy discomfort because of your love for maintaining comfort.

Examples:  Not doing pushups when my phone notification prompts me.  Skipping the cold plunge.  Choosing dessert instead of salad.  Snoozing my early morning alarm.



**SUFFERING**

Principle:  "Discomfort is inevitable, but suffering is optional."  If you are okay with the discomfort, then it's not suffering.

Examples:  After your neighbor is in a devastating car accident, mowing their lawn is hardly a sacrifice.  After 100 finger-pricks, you know the sensation will be over quickly and it's not worth dreading.  Buying an engagement ring doesn't prompt self-pity over a painful financial loss.  After whatever discomfort happens, you move on to the next thing (mentally & emotionally).



**RESPONDING RIGHTLY = MATURITY**

Principle:  "Agonizing about the water temperature doesn't make the cold plunge any warmer."  Blame your desire-for-comfort for holding you back.  If you don't ignore it or overcome it, it will cause you to make immature decisions.

Examples:  Will I ignore my fatigue and get up when my alarm goes off?  Will I use chemicals to self-medicate negative emotions? After getting a paycheck, will I splurge on rewarding myself or put money into my savings account? If someone says something to push my buttons, will I snap back?



**RIGHT START = GOOD ENDINGS**

Principle:  The primary challenge happens at the starting line.  Failure happens due to overthinking and excuses.  If you can start, you've feel ready to finish.

Examples:  If you get in the car to go to the gym, finishing the workout will happen naturally.  If you can jump into the pool, then your body will get used to the water temperature after a few minutes.  If you get out of bed, you probably won't snooze your alarm. If you start driving 8 hours to visit relatives, you probably won't turn after 3 hours on the road.
